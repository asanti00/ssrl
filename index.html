Monitor and track Harmful Algal Blooms using Hyperspectral Imaging Technology

Mission

AquaLens will provide unprecedented accessibility to real-time HAB monitoring, empowering local governments and environmental organizations to make informed decisions on water quality management. The mission will also serve as an educational platform, allowing students to engage in satellite operations, data analysis, and environmental policy discussions. This mission aligns with global efforts to combat water pollution and climate change, showcasing the potential of university-led CubeSat programs in tackling real-world environmental challenges.

Key Mission Components

  • Hyperspectral Camera: The primary payload is a high-resolution hyperspectral sensor capable of detecting the spectral signatures of phytoplankton pigments, such as chlorophyll-a, phycocyanin, and other key indicators of HABs.
  • AI-Powered Onboard Processing: AquaLens will utilize edge computing technology to process images in space, reducing the need for extensive data transmission and enabling real-time detection of HABs.
  • Data Downlink & Open-Source Platform: The CubeSat will transmit processed images and raw spectral data to a university-operated ground station, where it will be made publicly available through an open-source platform for environmental agencies and researchers.
  • Sun-Synchronous Orbit (SSO): The satellite will be placed in a 500-600 km sunsynchronous orbit, allowing consistent daily imaging of targeted water bodies.
